NEPRA 2024 Performance Evaluation

Regulatory Analysis

The NEPRA Performance Evaluation highlights Pakistan's transmission network's operational challenges, including overloaded corridors, weak voltage regulation, and project delays. These issues result in reduced power transfer efficiency and increased reliance on costly RFO plants. The proposed solution involves adding series compensation to transmission lines, completing key infrastructure like Lahore North G/S, and improving load evacuation. These steps aim to enhance system stability, reduce costs, and ensure efficient power distribution.

NEPRA Grid Congestion

NTDC Transmission System Expansion Plan (TSEP)

System Planning

The main issue in the TSEP 2024-34 is the integration of renewable energy, particularly due to its intermittent nature, leading to voltage instability and reactive power compensation challenges. Insufficient transmission infrastructure and N-1 reliability concerns exacerbate these issues. Proposed solutions include reinforcing the grid with 500 kV lines, STATCOMs, and synchronous condensers. Strengthening energy storage systems and smart grids is essential for reliable grid operation.
